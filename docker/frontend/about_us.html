About Us

Our Story

Founded in 1960, Adelaide Fringe began as a small alternative arts festival and has grown to become the second-largest annual arts festival in the world, after the Edinburgh Festival Fringe.

For over six decades, Adelaide Fringe has been transforming the streets and venues of Adelaide into a vibrant cultural hub each February and March. What started as a collection of small performances has evolved into a massive celebration featuring over 1,200 events across 300+ venues.

As an open-access festival, Adelaide Fringe has always embraced the spirit of inclusivity and diversity. Unlike curated festivals, anyone with a show, exhibition, or event is welcome to register and participate, making it a true celebration of artistic freedom and expression.

Our Mission

Adelaide Fringe is committed to:

  • Supporting and showcasing independent artists from Australia and around the world
  • Creating accessible platforms for artistic expression and innovation
  • Fostering cultural diversity and community engagement
  • Providing economic opportunities for artists and the wider community
  • Positioning Adelaide as a global cultural destination

Festival Impact

Adelaide Fringe has a significant impact on South Australia's cultural landscape and economy:

  • Contributing over $170 million to the South Australian economy annually
  • Creating thousands of jobs in arts, hospitality, and tourism sectors
  • Attracting over 3.5 million attendees each year
  • Supporting over 7,000 artists across all disciplines
  • Providing a launching pad for emerging artists to develop their careers

Our Team

Adelaide Fringe is made possible by a dedicated team of staff, board members, volunteers, and supporters who work year-round to bring this incredible festival to life.

Our team's passion for the arts and commitment to supporting artists drives everything we do, from programming and production to marketing and community engagement.

Adelaide Fringe also relies on the generous support of our sponsors, partners, and the enthusiastic Adelaide audience who embrace the festival each year.
